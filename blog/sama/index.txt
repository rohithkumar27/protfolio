<li>most people start wanting money</li>
<li>many end up wanting to build something meaningful</li>
<li>outlier success usually comes after baseline success</li>
<li>these ideas still apply to almost everyone</li>
</ul>
<h2>1. compound yourself</h2>
<ul>
<li>compounding is magic</li>
<li>look for exponential growth, not linear progress</li>
<li>choose careers and projects where learning and impact accelerate over time
<ul>
<li>avoid paths where 2 years = 20 years of experience</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>use leverage: capital, technology, brand, networks, people</li>
<li>aim to add another zero to your definition of success
<ul>
<li>let go of small opportunities for step-change ones</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>think long-term when others don’t
<ul>
<li>trust the exponential, be patient</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>2. have almost too much self-belief</h2>
<ul>
<li>self-belief is a force multiplier
<ul>
<li>most successful people believe in themselves almost to delusion</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>strong conviction enables contrarian thinking</li>
<li>managing morale requires confidence
<ul>
<li>ambition attracts resistance</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>balance self-belief with self-awareness
<ul>
<li>treat criticism as potentially true, then decide</li>
<li>truth-seeking separates confidence from delusion</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>3. learn to think independently</h2>
<ul>
<li>original thinking is rarely taught
<ul>
<li>schools reward conformity, not creativity</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>think from first principles</li>
<li>generate ideas and test them quickly in the real world</li>
<li>failure is expected; being right once matters
<ul>
<li>grit comes from solving “unsolvable” problems repeatedly</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>4. get good at sales</h2>
<ul>
<li>belief alone is not enough</li>
<li>all great careers involve persuasion
<ul>
<li>you must sell to customers, hires, investors, media</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>clear thinking leads to clear communication
<ul>
<li>written communication is especially valuable</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>selling what you believe in feels right</li>
<li>sales is learnable with practice
<ul>
<li>show up in person when it matters</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>5. make it easy to take risks</h2>
<ul>
<li>people overestimate risk and underestimate reward</li>
<li>early career is the best time to take risks</li>
<li>aim for asymmetric bets: lose 1x, win 100x</li>
<li>comfort makes risk-taking harder
<ul>
<li>avoid lifestyle inflation</li>
<li>keep life cheap and flexible when possible</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>6. focus</h2>
<ul>
<li>focus multiplies effort</li>
<li>choosing the right problem matters more than hours worked
<ul>
<li>most work people do doesn’t matter</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>once priorities are clear, execute aggressively
<ul>
<li>speed correlates strongly with success</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>7. work hard</h2>
<ul>
<li>smart or hard gets you to top 10%</li>
<li>smart and hard gets you to top 1%</li>
<li>extreme effort produces extreme results
<ul>
<li>momentum compounds</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>meaningful work is often energizing</li>
<li>working hard is not shameful</li>
<li>stamina predicts long-term success
<ul>
<li>work hardest early when compounding is strongest</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>avoid burnout by liking the work and the people</li>
</ul>
<h2>8. be bold</h2>
<ul>
<li>hard problems attract better people</li>
<li>ambition creates tailwinds</li>
<li>don’t downscale your goals to match trends</li>
<li>follow curiosity
<ul>
<li>exciting problems attract support</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>9. be willful</h2>
<ul>
<li>the world bends more than people expect
<ul>
<li>most people don’t push hard enough</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>ask for what you want</li>
<li>rejection is normal
<ul>
<li>persistence creates room for luck</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>optimism is required
<ul>
<li>pessimists rarely achieve extreme success</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>10. be hard to compete with</h2>
<ul>
<li>scarcity creates value
<ul>
<li>if others can do what you do, they eventually will</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>build leverage through:
<ul>
<li>personal relationships</li>
<li>personal brand</li>
<li>cross-domain expertise</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>avoid mimetic behavior
<ul>
<li>differentiation matters</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>11. build a network</h2>
<ul>
<li>great work requires teams</li>
<li>networks limit or expand what you can do</li>
<li>help people generously over time</li>
<li>take care of collaborators
<ul>
<li>share upside generously</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>push people to grow, not to burn out</li>
<li>define yourself by strengths, not weaknesses
<ul>
<li>hire complementary people</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>look for rate of improvement, not credentials
<ul>
<li>ask: is this person a force of nature?</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>spend time with people who support your ambitions</li>
</ul>
<h2>12. you get rich by owning things</h2>
<ul>
<li>salaries rarely create extreme wealth</li>
<li>ownership creates upside
<ul>
<li>equity scales; time does not</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>own things that grow rapidly in value</li>
<li>build things people want at scale</li>
</ul>
<h2>13. be internally driven</h2>
<ul>
<li>external validation leads to safe, consensus paths
<ul>
<li>it distorts risk assessment</li>
<li>smart people are especially vulnerable</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>internal motivation sustains long-term excellence
<ul>
<li>the best people aim to impress themselves</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>obsession is required for extreme success</li>
<li>success eventually becomes doing excellent work that matters to you</li>
</ul>
<h2>closing thought</h2>
<ul>
<li>opportunity is unevenly distributed</li>
<li>luck matters more than people admit</li>
<li>progress is still possible without privilege</li>
<li>long-term belief, effort, and persistence matter</li>
<li>start as early as you can</li>
</ul>
