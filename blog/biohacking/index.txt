e:T211c,<p>biohacking is about aligning with biology, not forcing extremes</p>
<p>it is mostly about removing friction, not adding hacks</p>
<p>consistency beats novelty every time</p>
<h3>core principles</h3>
<ul>
<li>fix sleep, light, food, movement, stress before supplements</li>
<li>measure only what you can act on</li>
<li>avoid injections, experimental drugs, or extreme interventions</li>
<li>favor habits with long human safety records</li>
<li>stop anything that worsens sleep, anxiety, or recovery</li>
</ul>
<h3>sleep (highest roi)</h3>
<ul>
<li>sleep is the foundation everything stacks on</li>
<li>fixed wake-up time > fixed bedtime</li>
<li>morning sunlight within 30–60 minutes of waking</li>
<li>no bright or overhead light 2–3 hours before bed</li>
<li>cool, dark, quiet bedroom</li>
<li>caffeine cutoff 8–10 hours before sleep</li>
<li>alcohol framed as a sleep disruptor, not relaxation</li>
</ul>
<h4>circadian rhythm</h4>
<ul>
<li>light is the primary biological clock</li>
<li>outdoor daylight beats light boxes when possible</li>
<li>dim evenings support melatonin release</li>
<li>late-night screens = artificial jet lag</li>
<li>consistent meal timing reinforces circadian alignment</li>
</ul>
<h3>nutrition</h3>
<ul>
<li>fewer rules, more signal</li>
<li>whole foods over supplements</li>
<li>protein sufficiency for muscle, satiety, glucose stability</li>
<li>fiber for gut and metabolic health</li>
<li>minimize ultra-processed foods rather than chasing superfoods</li>
<li>time-restricted eating used cautiously, not dogmatically</li>
<li>hydration guided by thirst + urine color</li>
</ul>
<h4>glucose awareness (without obsession)</h4>
<ul>
<li>glucose spikes are context-dependent, not moral failures</li>
<li>walking after meals improves glucose handling</li>
<li>pairing carbs with protein/fat blunts spikes</li>
<li>avoid anxiety-driven continuous tracking</li>
</ul>
<h3>exercise (the real backbone)</h3>
<h4>movement baseline</h4>
<ul>
<li>daily movement is non-negotiable</li>
<li>steps and light activity matter even if you train</li>
<li>long sitting blocks blunt metabolic health</li>
</ul>
<h4>resistance training / lifting</h4>
<ul>
<li>primary goal: preserve and build muscle mass</li>
<li>muscle = metabolic health, insulin sensitivity, longevity</li>
<li>2–4 sessions per week is sufficient</li>
<li>compound movements prioritized (squat, hinge, push, pull, carry)</li>
<li>progressive overload > novelty</li>
<li>form and consistency beat ego lifting</li>
</ul>
<h4>cardio</h4>
<ul>
<li>cardio is for heart, mitochondria, and brain</li>
<li>not all cardio is the same</li>
</ul>
<h5>zone 2 cardio</h5>
<ul>
<li>low to moderate intensity, conversational pace</li>
<li>improves mitochondrial density and fat oxidation</li>
<li>2–4 hours per week total</li>
<li>examples: brisk walking, cycling, easy jogging, rowing</li>
</ul>
<h5>high-intensity cardio</h5>
<ul>
<li>short, hard efforts raise cardiovascular ceiling</li>
<li>improves vo2 max</li>
<li>1–2 sessions per week max</li>
<li>excessive hiit = stress accumulation</li>
</ul>
<h4>vo2 max</h4>
<ul>
<li>one of the strongest predictors of lifespan and healthspan</li>
<li>reflects heart, lung, blood, and muscle integration</li>
<li>improves via intervals, hills, mixed endurance + strength</li>
<li>progress stalls without sleep and recovery</li>
</ul>
<h3>recovery</h3>
<ul>
<li>adaptation happens during rest, not training</li>
<li>sleep, calories, and protein enable gains</li>
<li>soreness is not a success metric</li>
</ul>
<h4>stress &#x26; nervous system</h4>
<ul>
<li>chronic stress treated as a root cause</li>
<li>slow nasal breathing > complex breath protocols</li>
<li>regular time without digital input</li>
<li>social safety and trust affect biology</li>
</ul>
<h4>cold &#x26; heat exposure</h4>
<ul>
<li>cold exposure as mild hormetic stress, not fat-loss magic</li>
<li>short cold showers over extreme ice rituals</li>
<li>sauna for relaxation, cardiovascular support, sleep quality</li>
<li>recovery prioritized over suffering</li>
</ul>
<h3>fat loss (honest framing)</h3>
<ul>
<li>fat loss requires a sustained energy deficit</li>
<li>exercise supports fat loss, diet adherence matters more</li>
<li>muscle preservation is the priority while cutting</li>
</ul>
<h3>extended fasting (strategic use only)</h3>
<ul>
<li>extended fasting can reduce fat mass and improve insulin sensitivity</li>
<li>higher risk, higher reward tool</li>
<li>not required for most people</li>
</ul>
<h4>who it may suit</h4>
<ul>
<li>metabolically unhealthy individuals</li>
<li>people with significant fat to lose</li>
<li>those with prior fasting experience</li>
</ul>
<h4>who should avoid</h4>
<ul>
<li>underweight individuals</li>
<li>eating disorder history</li>
<li>high training volume athletes</li>
<li>poor sleep or high stress baselines</li>
</ul>
<h4>protocol basics</h4>
<ul>
<li>typically 36–72 hours, not weeks</li>
<li>water intake is mandatory</li>
<li>activity volume reduced significantly</li>
<li>heavy lifting paused</li>
</ul>
<h4>supplements &#x26; electrolytes during extended fasting</h4>
<ul>
<li>sodium: prevents dizziness and low blood pressure</li>
<li>potassium: supports nerve and muscle function</li>
<li>magnesium: reduces cramps, supports sleep and stress</li>
<li>electrolytes must be sugar-free</li>
</ul>
<h4>optional</h4>
<ul>
<li>black coffee or plain tea (cautiously)</li>
<li>bone broth (technically breaks fast but improves safety)</li>
</ul>
<h4>rules</h4>
<ul>
<li>do not stack extended fasting with intense training</li>
<li>fasting days: walking or light zone 2 only</li>
<li>fed days: lift heavy, train hard</li>
</ul>
<h3>supplements (minimalist, focused list)</h3>
<h4>core vitamins</h4>
<ul>
<li>vitamin d: immune, mood, bone health</li>
<li>vitamin b12: nerve function, energy metabolism</li>
<li>folate (b9): cell division, neurological health</li>
<li>vitamin c: antioxidant, collagen synthesis</li>
<li>vitamin a (food preferred): vision, immune health</li>
<li>vitamin e: antioxidant support</li>
<li>vitamin k2: calcium regulation, bone and vascular health</li>
</ul>
<h4>minerals</h4>
<ul>
<li>magnesium (glycinate or threonate): sleep, stress, muscle relaxation</li>
<li>zinc: immune and hormone function</li>
<li>iron: oxygen transport (supplement only if deficient)</li>
<li>iodine: thyroid support</li>
<li>selenium: thyroid enzymes, antioxidant systems</li>
<li>potassium: electrolyte balance (prefer food sources)</li>
</ul>
<h4>fatty acids</h4>
<ul>
<li>omega-3s (epa/dha): cardiovascular, brain, inflammation balance</li>
</ul>
<h4>protein &#x26; amino acids</h4>
<ul>
<li>adequate dietary protein first</li>
<li>protein powders for convenience only</li>
<li>creatine monohydrate: strength, cognition, aging support</li>
<li>glycine: sleep quality, connective tissue support</li>
</ul>
<h4>gut support</h4>
<ul>
<li>fiber from food first</li>
<li>probiotics short-term, strain-specific</li>
<li>prebiotics to support existing microbiome</li>
</ul>
<h4>rules</h4>
<ul>
<li>food first, supplements last</li>
<li>test when possible, guess less</li>
<li>avoid stacking dozens of compounds</li>
</ul>
<h3>mental performance</h3>
<ul>
<li>attention is a finite resource</li>
<li>single-tasking beats productivity hacks</li>
<li>deep work protected by environment design</li>
<li>digital minimalism as cognitive hygiene</li>
</ul>
<h4>environment</h4>
<ul>
<li>reduce constant noise and notification load</li>
<li>clean air and ventilation matter more than gadgets</li>
<li>daylight exposure at workspaces</li>
<li>ergonomic basics over expensive setups</li>
</ul>
<h3>tracking &#x26; wearables</h3>
<ul>
<li>tools for pattern recognition, not self-surveillance</li>
<li>trends > daily fluctuations</li>
<li>sleep regularity > sleep scores</li>
<li>stop tracking if it increases anxiety</li>
</ul>
<h3>what fades</h3>
<ul>
<li>extreme fasting as a lifestyle</li>
<li>biohacking as identity or flex</li>
<li>optimization without trade-off awareness</li>
<li>novelty chasing</li>
</ul>
<h3>what wins</h3>
<ul>
<li>boring routines done daily</li>
<li>recovery as performance</li>
<li>muscle + heart + mitochondria as priorities</li>
<li>sustainability over intensity</li>
</ul>
<h3>bottom line</h3>
<p>the best biohack is not adding more</p>
<p>it is removing friction, noise, and self-inflicted stress</p>
<p>lift weights, protect sleep, move daily, recover hard</p>
